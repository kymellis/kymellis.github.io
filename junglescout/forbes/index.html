Using the sophisticated analytics to mine big data's dividends.

Creating the unicorns of tomorrow.

Edited by Bruce Upbin, Steven Bertoni, Shelby Carpenter and Forbes Technology Team

Greg Mercer, 28

Founder & CEO, Jungle Scout, Jump Send, Fetcher, Splitly

Despite exhibitiing an entrepreneurial streak from a young age, Greg Mercer was always risk-averse. Which makes sense, considering his startup Jungle Scout, helps ecommerce entrepreneurs use data to easily find great products to sell on Amazon that will verifiably succeed. Like many great startups, Mercer developed Jungle Scout to address his own personal challenges of finding new product ideas to sell on Amazon. Continuing to find ideas for his $400 per month physical products business wasn't easy, and he knew he wasn't alone.

It turns out he was right. After building a simple prototype, Mercer has more than validated the need for an Amazon product research tool: 20 months after launch, the company is grossing over $1MM per month, has built a rapid fan base of more than 45,000 customers, and has the likes of Rich Dad's Robert Kiyosaki calling it "amazing" in his personal recommendations."Entrepreneurship is addictive. And contagious. I seriously believe that within the next 10 years, another Forbes 30 Under 30 winner will credit Jungle Scout and our instructional content as the entry point for them starting a hugely profitable ecommerce business."

  • All-Star Alumni

    At our fifth annual celebration of young movers and shakers, we see what some of the most influential FORBES 30 Under 30s from years past have been up to.

  • Mark Zuckerberg

    Facebook, 2011

    Everyone’s favorite social networker welcomed a baby girl on Dec 1st and is beginning fatherhood with a 2 month paternal leave from Facebook. Another milestone for the already accomplished 31 year old. As you have probably read, he and his wife plan to give away 99% of their shares in Facebook over their lifetimes.

  • Solomon Hykes

    Docker, 2011

    Hykes's bold moves and high risks have worked thus far for the former DotCloud founder, who was saved when he and Ben Golub joined forces on Docker. Docker unique open-sourcing format has attracted compaines as huge as Microsoft and CoreOS to use its container platform.

  • Joshua Reeves

    Gusto, 2012

    Since we put him on the 30 Under 30 list in 2012, the serial entrepreneur and CEO of Gusto (formerly known as ZenPayroll) has raised $80 million, including a $60 million round from Google Capital in April 2015. Gusto is branching beyond managing paychecks for small businesses into health benefits and workers’ compensation plans. Gusto now has more than 25,000 customers, and 300 employees across their San Francisco and Denver offices.
